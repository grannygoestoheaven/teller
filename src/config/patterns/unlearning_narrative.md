---
**"Unlearning {{Topic}}:**
Write a 4-sentence unlearning narrative about **{{Topic}}** that **destroys a common myth** by:
1. **Starting with a provocative reframe** (e.g., 'Power isn’t a crown—it’s a debt.'),
2. **Citing hard evidence or historical patterns** (e.g., '78% of "compliant" employees undermine toxic leaders passively [Study, Year].'),
3. **Exposing the hidden cost or paradox** (e.g., 'The higher you climb, the more you rely on those you exploit.'),
4. **Ending with a brutal question** that forces personal reflection (e.g., 'Still feel powerful?').

**Structure:**
1. **Assumption:** '[Common Belief].'
   **Reality:** '[Contradictory Truth] + [Evidence/Example].'
2. **Assumption:** '[Second Belief].'
   **Reality:** '[Contradictory Truth] + [Evidence/Example].'
3. **Assumption:** '[Third Belief].'
   **Reality:** '[Contradictory Truth] + [Evidence/Example].'

**Unlearning Challenge:**
'[Direct, personal question that forces the reader to confront their own stance.]'

---
**Rules:**
- **No softening.** Use blunt, visceral language.
- **No abstractions.** Anchor every claim in **data, history, or psychology**.
- **End with a gut-punch question** that implies the reader’s complicity or vulnerability.
- **Tone:** Cynical, urgent, and **unapologetic**. Assume the reader’s beliefs are fragile—then shatter them.

**Example Topics:**
- Unlearning 'Power' output:
"Power isn’t a crown—it’s a debt. Every order you give, every decision you enforce, is a withdrawal from the trust bank of those beneath you. The myth? That power is yours to keep. The truth? It’s a loan, and the interest is paid in loyalty, fear, or revolt. History’s most "powerful" figures—CEOs, dictators, even parents—collapse when the ledger turns red. Ask the fallen: What’s left when the people you ruled stop believing in the system? Spoiler: Not a legacy. Just an empty throne."

- 'Unlearning 'Moon' output:
"The Moon is not a passive satellite. Its surface records a history of bombardment that reshaped Earth’s early chemistry and climate. Its gravitational influence doesn’t just create tides—it stabilizes Earth’s axial tilt, preventing chaotic climate shifts. The "dark side" is a misnomer; it receives equal sunlight, but its composition differs radically from the near side due to asymmetric cooling and impact history. Re-evaluate: How does a body with no atmosphere or geologic activity still dictate fundamental patterns of life on Earth?"

---

### **Key Triggers for the Model:**
- **"Raw"** = Signal for confrontational tone.
- **"Cite hard evidence"** = Force the use of studies, stats, or historical examples.
- **"Gut-punch question"** = Ensure the ending is personal and disruptive.
- **"No abstractions"** = Ban vague language; demand concrete contradictions.
